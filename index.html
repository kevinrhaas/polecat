Everyday advice
“What's the secret to extra-crispy roast potatoes?”
Claude · Opus paid

Parboil until just tender, then shake to rough up the edges — the craggy surface crisps. Roast in hot fat at ~220°C / 425°F.

Gemini · Pro paid

A pinch of baking soda in the boiling water roughens the surface. Drain, shake, then roast in plenty of hot oil.

Llama 3.3 70B free

Boil 8–10 min, dry well and fluff the edges, roast in very hot fat, and don't crowd the tray.

✦ Consensus Comprehensive

Parboil until just-tender (a pinch of baking soda in the water helps), drain and shake hard to rough up the edges, then roast uncrowded in plenty of hot fat at ~220°C/425°F, turning once. The fluffy, craggy surface plus hot fat is what gives that shatter-crisp crust — the consensus blends every tip into one method.

Caught a wrong answer
“Can I swap baking soda for baking powder, 1-to-1?”
Model A ✗ off

Sure — they're both leaveners, so swap them 1:1.

Model B ✓

No — baking soda is ~3–4× stronger and needs an acid; a 1:1 swap tastes soapy and collapses.

Model C ✓

Not 1:1. Use about ¼ as much soda plus an acid — or just use the baking powder.

✦ Consensus Validated Synthesis

No — not a 1:1 swap. Baking soda is much stronger and needs an acidic ingredient to work; use roughly ¼ the amount plus an acid (like cream of tartar), or simply use baking powder as written. Polecat flagged the lone “just swap it” answer as wrong and kept the validated guidance — exactly the kind of confident mistake that ruins a bake.

Free vs paid, head-to-head
“Explain compound interest simply, with an example.”
Llama 3.3 70B free

Interest earns interest. $1,000 at 5%/yr → $1,050 after year 1; year 2 you earn 5% on $1,050 = $1,102.50. It snowballs.

Claude · Opus paid

Same core, plus nuance: it grows faster the more often it compounds (monthly > yearly), and starting early matters most.

✦ Consensus Comprehensive

Compound interest is interest earning interest. Example: $1,000 at 5%/yr is $1,050 after one year, then $1,102.50 after two (you earn 5% on the new balance). It snowballs — faster the more often it compounds, and the earlier you start. The free model nailed the basics; the paid one added the nuance — together you get a complete, trustworthy answer.
